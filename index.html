Research Project

  • Automatical heterogeneous bugs detection of HPC applications - HPCTest

    High-Performance Computing (HPC) is essential for running critical applications such as stock market analysis, weather forecasting, drug discovery, and more. Heterogeneous bugs are a particular concern in these applications; they cause program outputs to vary across different computing backends. Such bugs in HPC applications can adversely affect human decision-making and may even lead to loss of life. Therefore, detecting heterogeneous bugs is crucial. In our research project, we have developed an automated tool named HPCTest, designed to detect heterogeneous bugs specifically in a scientific application called LAMMPS. HPCTest starts by generating initial seed inputs for each LAMMPS example, utilizing a Large Language Model for this purpose. It then dynamically analyzes LAMMPS to extract subsystems relevant to each example. To generate a diverse range of inputs, HPCTest employs AFL++ for fuzzing these initial seed inputs, which is guided by outputs of backends and kernel-sensitive variables. If outputs from different backends are different, there are potential bugs.
